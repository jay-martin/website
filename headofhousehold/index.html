Highlights

Head of household is a filing status for single parents* who maintain their own household.* People who file as heads of households receive a larger standard deduction and preferential tax brackets compared to single filers.

Itemized deductions reduce the benefit of the HOH filing status because much of its benefit derives from the larger HOH standard deduction compared to filing as single.

Yes. The standard deduction and tax brackets for married joint filers are set at roughly double those of single filers, while heads of households are set about 1.5x times single filers.
