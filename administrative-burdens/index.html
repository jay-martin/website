Countable Income
4a.
Countable Unearned Income: You may exclude the first $20 of unearned income from your countable unearned income. If line 3d is equal to or exceeds $20, subtract $20 from line 3d and input the result. If line 3d is less than $20, input $0; the remainder will be used in the next line.
$
4b.
If your unearned income is less than $20, you may use the remainder of the $20 exclusion from line 4a towards your countable earned income. Subtract the remainder you determined when calculating line 4a from line 3a and input the result. If the remainder was $0, input the earned income you reported in line 3a.
$
4c.
You may exclude the first $65 of earned income you made this month from your countable earned income. If value of line 4b is equal to or exceeds $65, subtract $65 from line 4b and input the result. If line 4b is less than $65, input $0.
$
4d.
You may exclude impairment related work expenses from your countable earned income. Subtract any impairment related work expenses from line 4c and input the result. If the value is less than $0, input $0.
$
4e.
Countable Earned Income: You may exclude 50% of your remaining earned income. Divide line 4d by 2 and input the result.
$
4f.
Countable Income (Not Blind): Add line 4a to 4e and input the result.
$
4g.
Are you either of the following: (1) blind and under the age of 65; or (2) blind and over the age of 65 and you received SSI in the month before you turned 65 on account of your blindness?
4h.
Countable Income (Blind): You may exclude reasonable, unreimbursed work expenses that you needed to pay as a result of being blind. Subtract blind work expenses from line 4f and input the result. If the value is less than $0, input $0.
